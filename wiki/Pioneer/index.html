Pioneer

is a function performed by a plant which is able to grow in conditions unfavorable to others and in so doing improve the environmental conditions to a degree that they are able to sustain life which could previously not colonise that environment.


A-Z of plants which function as a Pioneer

A

  • Acer pseudoplatanus (Sycamore)
  • Acer rubrum (Red Maple)
  • Alnus cordata (Italian Alder)
  • Alnus glutinosa (Alder)
  • Alnus rubra (Red Alder)
  • Alnus rugosa (Speckled Alder)
  • Alnus sinuata (Sitka Alder)
  • Alnus tenuifolia (Mountain Alder)
  • Arctostaphylos uva-ursi (Bearberry)

B

  • Betula ermanii (Gold Birch)
  • Betula papyrifera (Paper Birch)
  • Betula pendula (Silver Birch)
  • Betula populifolia (Grey Birch)
  • Betula pubescens (White Birch)

B cont.

  • Buddleia paniculata

H

  • Haloxylon ammodendron
  • Haloxylon persicum (Salt Tree)

L

  • Larix decidua (Larch)
  • Lupinus arboreus (Tree Lupin)

P

  • Populus balsamifera (Balsam Poplar)
  • Populus deltoides (Eastern Cottonwood)
  • Populus grandidentata (Canadian Aspen)
  • Populus tremuloides (American Aspen)
  • Prunus pensylvanica (Pin Cherry)
  • Prunus spinosa (Sloe)
  • Prunus virginiana (Chokecherry)

R

  • Rhus glabra (Smooth Sumach)
  • Rubus fruticosus (Blackberry)

S

  • Salix alba (White Willow)
  • Salix amygdaloides (Peach Leaved Willow)
  • Salix aurita (Eared Sallow)
  • Salix bebbiana (Beak Willow)
  • Salix caprea (Goat Willow)
  • Salix cinerea (Grey Willow)
  • Salix exigua (Coyote Willow)
  • Salix fluviatilis (River Willow)
  • Sorbus aria (Whitebeam)
  • Sorbus decora (Showy Mountain Ash)
  • Sorbus sitchensis (Sitka Mountain Ash)

U

  • Ulex europaeus (Gorse)
