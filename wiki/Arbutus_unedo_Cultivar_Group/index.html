Uses

Edible uses

A list of parts of Arbutus unedo Cultivar Group with edible uses.

Fruit

Fresh

Round red fruits 1-2cm in diameter are produced in abundance in Autumn. It is often quoted that the name "unedo" comes from the Latin meaning "I eat one" [1]:430 [2]:43 [3] however this is attributed by some to a lack of flavour [4] [1]:430 and by others to the fruits potential to become quickly alcoholic after it has ripened, fermenting while still on the tree. Opinions on the fruits value as an edible crop vary. The fruit is variously described as subtle but sweet and with a tropical texture [2]:43 or conversely as bland and disappointing [1]:430 [4].

Jam

A jam or jelly can be made from the fresh red fruits. Care must be taken to pick the berries before they begin to ferment. For jelly, use a jellycloth to remove all seeds, or for a jam find a sieve or gauze with a mesh fine enough to remove the gritty seeds while allowing the majority of the pulp to pass through. The jam may ferment quickly once opened, so avoid large jars.

Alcohol

In Portugal a distilled sprit (a brandy) is made from the fruit of Arbutus unedo, called aguardente de medronhos (sometimes, medronheiro). Fruit is harvested between September and November and placed in open-topped barrels (vasilhas) and water added to start the fermentation. To prevent a second fermentation (i.e., vinegar) a layer of mud (adobe) is put over the top to exclude oxygen. After 3-4 months the mass is transferred to a copper still, boiled and evaporated alcohol condensed and collected. "Better qualities of medronho under go multiple distillations". Usually, at least in the province of Algarve, the medronho is sold to licensed bottling companies who mix it with alcohol and "various essences". [5]

Material uses

A list of parts of Arbutus unedo Cultivar Group with material uses.

Wood

Fuel
Firewood

Arbutus wood makes a good firewood, burning hot and long [6]. The bark can be used for tanning [7]:117.

Furniture making
Turning

The timber of Arbutus unedo is a fine-grained pink hardwood. It's often gnarled and twisted trunks limits it's use as a timber for construction [6], but it's close grain and unique pink colour may make it appropriate for fine woodworking [8].

Medicinal uses(Warning!)

A list of parts of Arbutus unedo Cultivar Group with medicinal uses.

Leaves

Astringent

The bark & leaves have been used historically for colds and stomach problems [6]. The leaves, bark and root are astringent and diuretic [9].

Seeds

Vasodialator

Cultivation

As a member of the Ericaceae family, Arbutus unedo thrives in acidic or neutral soils [1]:430 but, unique to its family, has been reported to tolerate alkalinity [9].

Crops

Fruit

Harvest

The fruit ripens in late autumn to water, producing an often abundant amount of fleshy red fruits 1-2cm in diameter. When red, often with an orange tint, soft, and easily removable from the tree, they are ripe. They can be hand picked but the even distribution of the fruit throughout the dense canopy can make this challenging. The fruit is very easily removed from the tree, however, and so can be harvested by laying out sheets on the ground surrounding it and shaking the branches. [7]:117 The fruits are very soft and not storable. They will quickly ferment, and indeed some may have already fermented on the tree, so consume or process quickly.

Processing

While edible fresh, most reports echo that are are not overly pleasant to eat in quantity owing to a gritty texture and variable flavour from one fruit to another: some sweet and pleasant, others bland. In Spain and Portugal they are often distilled into a liquor called Madronho and they can also make a pleasant tasting jam or jelly. In the latter case, it is advisable to strain the pulp of the fruit to remove the copious gritty seeds.
