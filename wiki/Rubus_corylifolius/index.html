Propagation

Seed - requires stratification and is best sown in early autumn in a cold frame. Stored seed requires one month stratification at about 3°c and is best sown as early as possible in the year. Prick out the seedlings when they are large enough to handle and grow on in a cold frame. Plant them out into their permanent positions in late spring of the following year.

Cuttings of half-ripe wood, July/August in a frame[3]. Tip layering in July. Plant out in autumn.

Division in early spring or just before leaf-fall in the autumn[3].

Cultivation

Easily grown in a good well-drained loamy soil in sun or semi-shade[4][5][3].

This species is now more usually seen as an aggregate and has been split up into a number of species. This name is no longer really used[6].

Plants in this genus are notably susceptible to honey fungus[3].
