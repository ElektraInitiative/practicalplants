Initial, the jargon.

Back link: this is where a internet site links to your internet site i.e. Web site X has a link pointing to your web site, so your site has a back link from Site X

A single-way link: exactly where only internet site X links to your web site, but your website does NOT link to website X

Reciprocal link: exactly where two websites have swapped links so they each link to every single other

Anchor text: the text/description that is associated with your link on the back link site.

Page rank: Google's proprietary system for assigning a worth (from 1-10 exactly where 10 is best) to the combined quantity and the good quality of back links pointing to your website. Often abbreviated to "PR"

Search Engine Results Page (SERP): we use the terms "SERPs" to refer to the search engine results page, and the position of a internet site on it. So if a website has a SERP of 3, it indicates for a distinct keyword search it appears on page three of the results. Note that some individuals use SERP to refer to the position on the page, so that a SERP of three might also mean the site appears at #3 on the initial page.

In the on the web marketing globe, Page Rank implies a lot. Growing a website's PR is 1 of the significant foci of on-line marketing and advertising.

The aim is to get as high a PR as feasible for your web site. PR is measured by a proprietary algorithm belonging to Google. What Google does is measure the high quality of the web site linking to your website. How does it do this? By measuring, amongst other factors, that site's PR. There is also evidence that the degree of relatedness of content material is also essential. If you internet site is an e.g. True estate website, then a back link from a site dealing in scuba gear might not be worth a lot to you. But back links from sites that have great PR and are associated to you (e.g. by business or by region/place) are properly worth pursuing. By increasing the quantity and high quality of links pointing to your website, over time you will boost your PR.

Note also that a lot of other engines also use back links as portion of their SERP algorithm. Some may possibly not be as sophisticated as Google, but several measure the number of back links in their index and use this as portion of how they rank your website beneath a certain search.
